Jane Eyre

Jane Eyre tells the story of an orphan named Jane. Jane is raised by her hateful aunt, and loved by nobody. Jane is a woman who thinks and doesn't want to be trapped by what is expected of her. Although she is described as a woman with no beauty, she is still a wonderful and smart person. When she takes a job working as a governess for Mr. Rochester, she didn't expect to find love. Unfortunately, many things stand in their way, and a happy ending isn't always secure.

Charlotte Brontë

'I would always rather be happy than dignified.'-Charlotte Brontë

Charlotte Brontë was born April 21, 1816. She was a bold writer and strong feminist for her time. Charlotte came from a family of writers, with her sisters Emily and Anne also being well known names. While Anne was more of a poet, and Emily became famous for her book "Wuthering Heights" which varies greatly to "Jane Eyre". Unfortunately, although Charlotte wrote a story of love, she unfortunately never found it. She died at the age of 35, single.
